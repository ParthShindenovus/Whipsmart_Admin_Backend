Q: How does GST work on vehicle purchases under a novated lease?

===Q&A-003===
Q: What is the residual (balloon) calculation for a novated lease?  
A: The residual (balloon) value for a novated lease is stipulated by the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and depends on the length of the lease. The minimum residual values are:  
- 1-year lease: 65.63% of the car's initial value  
- 2-year lease: 56.25%  
- 3-year lease: 46.88%  
- 4-year lease: 37.50%  
- 5-year lease: 28.13%  
A high-kilometer deduction may be applied if driving more than 25,000 km per year.

===Q&A-004===
Q: How are pay deductions and invoicing handled in a novated lease?  
A: Each month, WhipSmart invoices the employer and direct debits the agreed amount of the lease, including GST. The employer then applies a deduction to the employee's pay template and deducts the GST-free amount pre-tax. WhipSmart provides a deduction schedule to align the monthly invoice with weekly or fortnightly pay cycles. If the vehicle is FBT-exempt (e.g., an electric vehicle), all deductions are taken pre-tax. If the vehicle incurs an FBT charge, a portion of the deduction is taken post-tax to account for the fringe benefits tax.

===Q&A-015===
Q: What is FBT reporting, and how does it relate to novated leasing?  
A: FBT reporting refers to the process where employers provide a Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) report to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) for the FBT year, which runs from 1 April to 31 March. The percentage of business use claimed for the vehicle reduces the FBT owed. If the vehicle is FBT-exempt, such as an electric vehicle, no FBT will be due.

===Q&A-017===
Q: What are the options at the conclusion of a novated lease?  
A: At the end of a novated lease, the employee has the following options:  
1. Sell or trade the car. If the sale price (including GST) is more than the residual price, the employee can retain the difference. If it is less, the employee pays the difference.  
2. Re-lease the car and retain tax benefits. The residual price (excluding GST) becomes the new vehicle price on which the new lease payments are calculated.  
3. Keep the car by paying off the residual price (including GST). Note: If the residual is paid out, GST is applied. If the car is re-leased, GST is not applied.
